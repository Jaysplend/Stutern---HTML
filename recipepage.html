Okpa (Bambara nut)

Okpa (pronounced á»Œkpa) is a delicacy prepared by the Igbo people with a special type of beans known as Bambara nuts. It is common in Enugu state and classified as a traditional Nigerian delicacy due to its uniqueness. The botanical name of this leguminous plant is Vigna Subterranea. Bambara nut originates from West African countries of Nigeria, Ghana and Ivory Coast. It is, however, interesting to note that while people of Enugu, Ebonyi and Kogi cultivate this crop, they are not the chief cultivators. The chief cultivators of this highly proteinous food are the people of Jos Plateau and Yola.

Ingredients and measurements

S/N Ingredients Measurements
1 Okpa flour 4 cups
2 Palm oil 10 tablespoons
3 Blended Fresh Pepper 2 tablespoons
4 Salt Add to taste
5 Water 4 Cups
6 Seasoning 2 cubes
7 Powdered Uziza seed 2 tablespoons

Method of Preparation

  • Sieve the okpa flour into a big bowl, salt, smashed seasoning cubes and mix.
  • Pour the oil into the mixture and mould with your hands until you achieve unity in colour, deep yellow.
  • Boil the water in a pot. It should not be steaming hot but rather lukewarm.
  • Slowly pour the water into the mixture while you knead. The quantity of water depends on how you want the end result to be. If very soft, add a generous amount, if on the fence of soft and hard, act accordingly.
  • Add the blended pepper and powdered uziza into the mixture and use a turning stick to stir not your hands. Set aside when ready.
  • Boil water in a pot. Ensure that the water is enough for the wrapped okpa to be completely soaked
  • Wrap the okpa in nylons into sizes of your choice and place in the pot of steaming water.
  • Allow boiling for at least an hour and serve hot.
